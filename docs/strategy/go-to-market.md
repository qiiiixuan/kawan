# Go-to-Market

> Hypothetical-but-credible beachhead. Specific names (which estate, which MP, which NGO) flagged TBD until the team pins partners. The sequence and channels are real even if the contacts aren't yet.

## Beachhead

**One mature HDB estate with a high concentration of less tech-savvy elderly residents living alone.**

Selection criteria for the pilot estate:

- ≥30% residents aged 65+ (CDC / SingStat data; older estates like Toa Payoh, Bukit Merah, Queenstown, mature Tampines precincts qualify).
- An MP / GRC team with a public stance on caregiver burden or eldercare access.
- An RC / RN that already runs welfare visits and would benefit from triage relief.
- An anchor NGO (SAC operator: TOUCH / Lion's Befrienders / NTUC Health / AWWA) already operating on the ground.
- Physical void-deck space with reliable mains power and Wi-Fi (or willingness to extend).

**Pilot footprint:** 1 kiosk in 1 void deck for 8 weeks. Success ≠ resident count; success = (a) 100+ unique kiosk sessions, (b) ≥15 escalated structured cases consumed by the MP volunteer team, (c) qualitative feedback from the MP volunteer team that the structured cases reduced manual triage time per case.

## First Users (three concentric rings)

We don't market to ring 1 directly — they don't read marketing.

**Ring 1 — Elderly residents (the actual end users).**

- Don't reach them via app stores, ads, or LifeSG. Reach them by **co-locating with where they already go**: void decks, RC offices, MPS queues, SAC drop-in centres, kopitiams.
- Adoption mechanism: a volunteer (RC member, RC visit volunteer, or NGO befriender) walks them through their first interaction. After that, they self-serve.

**Ring 2 — RC volunteers, MP volunteers, NGO befrienders (the gatekeepers).**

- These are the people who *introduce* the kiosk to ring 1. Their buy-in is the real GTM.
- Pitch to them: "When you do your weekly visit / Thursday session, the kiosk has already gathered the gist. You walk in with structured context. You spend more time helping, less time triaging."
- Acquisition: short workshop at the RC / NGO; live demo of the structured-case CSV being imported into whatever tool the MP team already uses.

**Ring 3 — MPs and GRC teams (the institutional buyers).**

- They authorise placement, wire the data flow into their tooling, and sponsor expansion.
- Pitch to them: "Triage relief for your volunteers. Structured-case throughput per Thursday session goes up because residents arrive pre-routed. Costs you nothing per session; reduces the volunteer hours needed."

## Acquisition Sequence

**Weeks 0–2: Pilot install.**

- Place 1 kiosk in 1 void deck (TBD estate).
- Onboard the local RC volunteer team in a 1-hour workshop.
- Co-design the MP volunteer dashboard view: CSV imported into whatever tool the MP team already uses (often Excel or Google Sheets).
- Soft-launch — no public announcement; let RC volunteers tell residents.

**Weeks 2–6: Iteration.**

- Weekly check-in with RC volunteers: which dialect coverage is weak? which agencies are missing from the directory?
- Weekly check-in with the MP volunteer team: are the structured cases usable? what fields are missing?
- Tune the agency directory and triage prompts based on actual cases.

**Weeks 6–8: Validation.**

- Measure (a) kiosk session count, (b) escalated case count, (c) volunteer hours saved (self-reported), (d) resident-reported helpfulness (verbal, captured by RC volunteers).
- Write a 4-page case-study deck for the next conversation.

**Weeks 8–12: Second-estate handshake.**

- Use the case study to approach a second MP / GRC team.
- Repeat the pilot pattern. Two estates running in parallel = the proof for institutional rollout.

## Channels

**Earned (primary at this stage).**

- MP / GRC referrals — the most credible channel. One MP recommending it to another beats any campaign.
- NGO networks — an SAC operator (TOUCH / Lion's Befrienders / NTUC Health / AWWA) referring across their footprint.
- Volunteer word-of-mouth among RC volunteers across estates.

**Owned.**

- Kiosk physical placement at void decks (the placement *is* the channel — residents discover it because it's where they already walk past).
- Receipt PDF — every escalated case prints a receipt with the case ID. Volunteers see the format every week and learn to expect it.

**Paid.**

- None for the first ~6 months. The unit economics don't justify ad spend at this stage; institutional adoption is the path.

## Adoption Triggers

The thing that gets a hesitant elderly resident to use it the first time is **a trusted volunteer walking them through it**. Not the kiosk itself.

The thing that gets the MP volunteer team to *keep* feeding cases through is the **time saved per case** vs the manual triage they do today.

The thing that gets the GRC team to authorise expansion is **a clear case study** (case count, volunteer hours saved, resident feedback) from the pilot estate.

## Risks

- **Volunteer resistance.** RC volunteers may see the kiosk as replacing their role rather than augmenting it. Mitigation: co-design the MP volunteer dashboard view with them; frame as triage relief, never as replacement.
- **Dialect coverage gaps.** SEALion / Workers AI Hokkien / Teochew quality may be uneven. Mitigation: kiosk gracefully falls back to Mandarin or English; transparent about limitations.
- **Privacy concerns.** "Kiosk listens" reads as surveillance to some. Mitigation: consent banner, no audio retention, clear copy, RC volunteers explain on first onboarding.
- **No real partner = no pilot.** The whole sequence depends on landing a first MP / GRC partnership. Without that, pivot the first deployment to an SAC drop-in centre — same flow, lower institutional ceiling but faster start.

## What "Won" Looks Like at 6 Months

- 5+ kiosks in 5+ void decks.
- 1,500+ kiosk sessions.
- 200+ escalated structured cases consumed by MP volunteer teams.
- 1 institutional buyer (PA / MSF / HDB / a GRC town council) signalling intent to fund expansion.
